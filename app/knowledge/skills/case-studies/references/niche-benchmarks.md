## Key reference math

- **Median MRR across paid Skool communities: $2,800–$4,200** (mid-2026)
- **Top decile (10% best-performing): > $25,000 MRR**
- **A-tier creators in $10K MRR range** typically run **3.2 paid cohorts/year**, each averaging **84 enrollments at a blended $167 price** → +$52K annualized cohort revenue on top of subscription line.
- **Churn:** Skool platform avg 8-12% monthly churn. Best-in-class: 4-6%. Sub-4% is exceptional.
- **Conversion from audience → member:** 0.3–2% across niches; 0.5–1% is the realistic working assumption.
- **Trial-to-paid conversion:** 15-25% on a 7-day Skool free trial, well-engineered.

## How to use these in pre-close output

1. **Always pair a real case with a benchmark range.** "Real Skool community in [niche] is doing X. The benchmark range for your audience size is Y–Z."
2. **Calibrate ambition to audience size.** If creator has 10K followers, the benchmark "median MRR target" is the realistic number to anchor on, not the top-decile.
3. **State assumptions.** "This assumes 0.8% audience-to-member conversion and 8% monthly churn — both achievable based on Skool's platform median."
4. **Never overpromise.** Always cite the LOW end of the median MRR range as the conservative case in the offer doc; the high end is the upside.
